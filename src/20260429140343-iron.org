Collected on planet surfaces and from asteroids.

Iron, Fe, atomic number 26. Melting point 1811K. Iron is one of the
most abundant heavier elements in the galaxy, primarily due to the
formation through fusion in massive stars of nickel-56, which decays
into an iron isotope. Although it isn't used as much in modern
metals it still has many uses in chemical production. It's also an
important element in medical molecular engineering applications
